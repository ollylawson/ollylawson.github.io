Notes from the Roof of the World: Afghanistan's Wakhan Corridor and Beyond

© Stuart Howarth 2019

Afghanistan's Wakhan Corridor is the gateway to the Little Pamir: one of the most remote inhabited places in the world. Sandwiched between China, Pakistan and Tajikistan at over 4,000 metres, the Little Pamir is home to Kyrgyz nomad settlements and the stunning Lake Chaqmaqtin. It is also about to change forever, as Chinese authorities begin work on a highway that will bring the valley hurtling into the 21st century.

Olly Lawson’s firsthand account—written in diary form—sheds light on a region rarely explored by Westerners. When he is let go from his job as a travel agent the day before departure, he suddenly finds the trip assuming an importance beyond mere adventure. Accompanied by two former colleagues, the trip sees them endure a grueling ten-day hike, nail-biting car journeys and an assortment of memorable characters.

With soaring descriptions of the landscape and snippets of philosophical musings, these travel notes will take you from leafy London to the Roof of the World in no time.
